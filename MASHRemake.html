M*A*S*H Remake

M*A*S*H is a game made originally for the Atari, where you play as a helicopter pilot looking to pick up wounded soldiers. Avoid trees on your route and in this remade version of the game, be prepared to change your plans at a minutes notice as lightning strikes threaten to hit your soldiers.

Gameplay

Development Process

This remake of the Atari game M*A*S*H was created in a single week, as an assignment for Game Design Studio 1. The main purpose of the creation of this game was to teach essential agile development skills, including the importance of task time estimations, user story mapping, and task prioritization , and especially their importance within a time pressured environment.

View GitHub Repo

Challenges in Development

The technical aspects of this project didn't pose any major difficulties, rather the major difficulties arose from applying industry standard practices for agile development:

Limited Development Time: As the development time given for this M*A*S*H remake was only a single week, difficulties arose as user stories and tasks needed to be prioritized so that the best final product could be made in the time allocated.


Task Time Estimations: Throughout it's development every task required to create the game was pre-recorded and given a time estimation. After the task was completed the actual time taken for the task was recorded so that the estimation and actual time could be compared.

Additional Feature Creation: Throughout the week, we were also tasked with ideating and implementing any additional feature that we liked that would add an additional gameplay element to the original M*A*S*H. My additional feature was a lightning bolt that targets any of the soldiers still remaining to be picked up. This means that the player may need to change their route quickly, possibly even needing to avoid picking up other soldiers first, as the helicopter can only carry 3 passengers at a time.
