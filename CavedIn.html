Caved In

Caved In is an online multiplayer, social deduction game, where players work together to collect samples in a randomly generated cave system. During their exploration players may get infected by hazards or enemies allowing them to place traps and sabotage cave systems, effecting other players. Try to navigate the cave systems, avoid getting infected and work with your team. But be careful of who you trust!

Development Process

Caved-In was made by a team of 4 programmers and 1 sound design student for our capstone project in Game Design Studio 2. The game was created using agile software development techniques over the course of 5, 2-3 week sprints. Each sprint required us to use project planning tools including Atlassian's Jira and Confluence, to record all SCRUM and reflective meeetings. Similarly to 'Primal Punch-Up' playtesting was held bi-weekly, with google forms allowing feedback to be collected. User stories were written at the start of each sprint based on this user feedback, allowing us to order goals by priority and size.

Challenges in Development

This project provided an array of new tasks and challenges for the team to overcome:

Online Multiplayer and Advanced Sessions: Creating and managing sessions to allow for players to connect and play with friends provided a new challenge. Similarly, there was much to be learnt about interactions between servers and clients particularly in Unreal Engine's game mode and state classes.

Polish and UI Animations: Sprint 5 focused on polishing the content that we had already implemented, giving us the chance to focus on the smaller details such as UI animations that help to provide a finished, professional feel.


Jira Backlog and Scope Creep: This project had a lot of big ideas at conception, that were not all able to be implemented in the time-frame of the subject. This required us to have to prioritize and order tasks, managing the time we did have to get a working game completed. Addtional content and fixes are currently planned, even post the subjects completion.

Blueprint Implementable Events: Learning to incorporate Unreals blueprint node system with C++ functions was challenging at first, but eventually allowed us to easily incorporate visual features, including model animations and UI that were called from code.
