Ethereal Enforcers (Prototype)

Ethereal Enforcers is a tech demo created in a team of 2 for an assignment in "Advanced Games Programming", a subject undertaken as part of my study at UTS. Ethereal Enforcers is a game you can play online with your friends, where you hunt a disappearing AI ghost with your vacuum gun in a procedurally generated map. Ring the bell when you think the ghost is near to reveal him, and try to catch him before he slips back into invisibility. Be careful not to ring the bell when he's too far away, else he'll be alerted to your location!

Development Process

As a part of the major assignment for this subject, we were tasked with creating a prototype game that used subsystems based on the content of the subject. This lead us to create Ethereal Enforcers, a prototype game, developed over a period of 4 weeks. The first two weeks we were each assigned a subsystem to create. For me it was the controller for the ghosts AI states and behaviour and for my team member it was the procedurally generated map. The next two weeks saw us having to integrate these subsystems together to create the tech demo. In the process, we were also required to refactor code to allow for online multiplayer through multicasting.

Challenges in Development

As an advanced subject of the degree, this assignment carried many challenges, namely:

AI States and Behaviour: Depending on player movement and interactions, and enemy health, the enumerator currentState of the enemyCharacter script will change. Depending on the AI state, the enemy will patrol, engage the player, evade, investigate, or guard an area using an A* algorithm.


Integration of Subsystems: Combining the AI and procedurally generated map subsystems provided new challenges in itself. For example: The test scene for the AI subsystem had nodes connected manually, and a single bell. This meant that the code for the procedurally generated map needed to be updated to accomodate for automatic node connections and random bell spawns.

Online Multiplayer: Online multiplayer was a new challenge as well. For multiplayer to be implemented, we needed to refactor code so that information could be passed to the server, and then to a multicast function that allows each client to have required information replicated.

Blueprint Animations: This was our first time using Unreal Engine and so learning a new editor layout, new terminology, and learning visual scripting in blueprints for animations and visual elements, took some time to understand.
